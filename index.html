Operational analysis

Three areas of initial focus

Based on CCAI's public work, team structure, and the JD for this role.

50+ volunteers · 5 continents

Volunteer coordination infrastructure

A well-designed volunteer CRM, onboarding and task / contribution tracking system would reduce contributor burden as well as allow smoother tracking of activities for CCAI staff.

4 programme streams · 23 projects

Programmatic impact tracking

Tracking the different impact metrics from grants, events, summer school and policy work and mapping those to the strategic direction of the organization is essential to improve decision-making around resource allocation. CCAI needs to be doing more of the most effective work so a reporting framework and dashboard of this kind would support that. It also connects to funder reporting, board-level strategic insight, and public-facing transparency.

501(c)(3) since 2021

Back-end infrastructure for scale

Reviewing the legal and financial structure and processes. Ensuring the foundational elements of operations are set up in a way that isn't creating friction for staff or core team. Bringing fresh eyes onto a legacy set-up can be helpful but this is as much a listening and learning focus area as an action one.

Relevant experience

What I bring to this

Mapping of my experience to the three operational opportunities above.

Volunteer CRM & coordination systems. At IKEA, I built a cross-country advocacy hub that took 30+ country teams from strategy education through campaign development to reporting — a SharePoint workflow designed around what teams actually needed, not what HQ assumed. The pattern is the same one CCAI needs: listen to distributed contributors, reduce friction, create structure without bureaucracy.

Programmatic impact dashboard. I have developed sustainability and social impact reporting frameworks for corporate clients that look at outputs, outcomes, and impact. I have created reporting dashboards for every program at Economist Impact to demonstrate performance and provide the insight for recommendations for future work. I have the knowledge, expertise, and experience to turn metrics into tangible actions.

Decision science lens. My current independent research applies multi-criteria decision analysis (AHP-TOPSIS) to sustainability materiality assessment — turning a subjective process into a structured one. I'd bring the same rigour to CCAI's resource allocation and programme prioritisation decisions.

Independent, remote, self-directed. I've worked independently across UK, European, and South African contexts for years. I'm used to operating autonomously in distributed teams, flagging issues early, and building my own structure. CCAI's remote-first, globally distributed model is how I already work.

First 90 days

How I'd approach the role

A sketch based on public information. The actual plan would be co-developed with the team in the first week.

Days 1–30

Learn

  • Deep listening with staff and core volunteers across all time zones
  • Audit existing processes, tools, documentation, and communication channels
  • Map what exists vs. what the JD needs to build — identify the real gaps, not the assumed ones
  • Understand funder reporting requirements and current compliance state
  • Identify 2–3 quick wins that build trust without creating disruption
Days 31–60

Design

  • Prioritise the 2–3 operational gaps with highest leverage
  • Propose CRM architecture for volunteer coordination and onboarding
  • Design impact tracking framework across programme streams
  • Draft standardised templates for grants administration and reporting
  • Present recommendations to the Executive Director with clear trade-offs
Days 61–90

Build

  • First operational system live — CRM pilot or reporting dashboard
  • Volunteer onboarding process documented and tested
  • Begin back-end infrastructure review (financial controls, procurement, contracts)
  • Establish regular coordination cadence with volunteer teams
  • Deliver first impact report to demonstrate the new framework

This is a sketch based on public information. The actual plan would be co-developed with the team in the first week — the best operational plans come from listening first, not arriving with answers.
